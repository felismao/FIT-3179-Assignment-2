Volcanic Eruption

There are more than 500 active volcanoes in the world. More than half of those volcanoes are part of the "Ring of Fire," a region that encircles the Pacific Ocean

Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) is a scale that describes the size of explosive volcanic eruptions base on magnitude and intensity. The most deadliest eruption is Mt Tambora in 1815, Indonesia. It has a VEI of 7.
