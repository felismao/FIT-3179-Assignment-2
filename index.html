Volcanic Events


There are more than 500 active volcanoes in the world. More than half of those volcanoes are part of the "Ring of Fire," a region that encircles the Pacific Ocean. Countries that are located in the ring of fire includes: Australia, New Zealand, Mexico, Peru, Chile, Guam, Taiwan, Philippines, Indonesia and Hawaii. Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) is a scale that describes the size of explosive volcanic eruptions base on magnitude, intensity, duration, and the plume. The scale is logarithmic from VEI 2 and up, meaning an increase of 1 index indicates an eruption that is 10 times as powerful. The most deadliest eruption is Mt Tambora in 1815, Indonesia. It has a VEI of 7.


Most Activity and Deaths

Stratovolcanoes have the highest number of volcanic activities. Stratovolcanoes have relatively steep sides and are cone-shaped. The lava produced by the stratovolcano does not flow that easily, hence it builds up around the vent forming the steep sides. Stratovolcanoes are also one of the most dangerous volcanoes as they are more likely to produce explosive eruptions due to gas building up in the viscous magma.

Mt. Pelee, a volcano at the northern end of Martinique, is one example of a stratovolcano. On May 8th, 1902 it erupts, causing over 28,000 deaths and destroyed the city of St. Pierre. This is the largest number of casualties for a volcanic eruption in this century.
